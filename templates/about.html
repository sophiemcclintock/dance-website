ABOUT

OUR STORY

When founder Molly Schaefer came to Samuel Marsden Collegiate School in 2019 she was very disappointed to discover the lack of dance throughout the school, so she decided to take it into her own hands, and start a dance club for everyone in the school to have a go, and have some fun during lunch.

From 2 people coming along to the classes, it has grown to around 25 and Molly and the rest of crew are still keen for it to grow even more. The biggest thing at Riptide is the relationships grown throughout the different year groups in the dance club, some of which still hold strong today. Come and join us in the dance studio today!

STAFF

Molly Schaefer
Molly is a Yr13 student and the founder of Riptide and has been passionate about dance from a very young age. She is part of the LA dance company and is currently involved in a range of styles , from tap and ballet to jazz. Teaching dance is one of her passions and she hopes to continue it for as long as she can.
Eva Tunnicliffe
Eva is also a Yr13 student and has been teaching dance alongside Molly since the start. She also trains at LA dance and has been dancing for 11 years and her favourite thing about it is the friends she has made along the way. She can teach all styles but specialises in jazz and she teaches once a week for Riptide.

COMPETITIONS

We offer 2-3 competitions a year for our students. These are great opportunities to develop your skills by watching some amazing performances and being judged on ways we can improve. The two competitions we are offering this year are Hip Hop Unite (HHU) and Impact Dance Experience and the details for both of these are below. We are aware that it can be a bit expensive so we do fundraising to ensure that all our dancers can compete in these competitions.


Hip Hop Unite
Date: 9-10th June
Cost: $65 per dancer

Impact Dance Experience
Date: 23rd-24th June
Cost: $10 per dancer

SHOWS

At the end of every year we always have a big end of year show which takes place in the Opera House. It is loads of fun and often the highlight of the year for a lot of our students. There is always a major theme, in previous years it has been stories such as Alice in Wonderland, the Lion Queen and the Sound of Music! We also sometimes have other performances throughout the year for our students to showcase what they have been learning, but these are on a much smaller scale.
